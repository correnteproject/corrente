corrente

Sharing cultural benefits obtained from a riparian ecosystem under restoration

 
 

Corrente is a project that addresses environmental pressures set by hydropower production on river courses of the italian province of Südtirol/Alto Adige. It aims at rendering tangible other benefits obtained from a local riparian ecosystem, namely the River Talfer/Talvera, which crosses the city of Bolzano; for locals to better perceive and give value to beneficial relations with a river that are not shaped by the industrial abstraction of water, and are rather based on reciprocal care. This is based on an ecological framework where rivers are understood beyond the resources, or provisioning services they provide.

Corrente is intended to question the adequacy of one’s consumption, the energy it demands, and the needs it meets within a framework of increasing demand on renewable energy sources. In turn, it exposes the way design can get intertwined in between local efforts to take care of rivers and the benefits they provide for local communities.

 
 

Based on the metaphore of a bladder that receives and releases water, a big transparent balloon was used as an artefact to collect and then spread in public places, the voice of river lovers and what their relation with the Talfer/Talvera is.

 
 

While listening to it, a play on the word energy is conveyed as a gift from the river’s current, for the listener to reflect on human wellbeing and their own response-ability with water.
 

Curious about this collective narration? Well, with a bit of help, the river can still sneak in virtual realms. Click on the balloon to play Corrente, immerse yourself in the waters of the river Talfer/Talvera, and let yourself appease your mind with the sound of the river and the voices of these water lovers. Needless to say, it sounds better with your eyes closed.

Making

 

Of

 

Exploration

 

Engaging waters

An emergency to locally and globally take care of water and the bodies that sustain it, led me to explore a relation of care with this liquid from a situated perspective. As a citizen of Bolzano, I played with the idea of displacement and language to reflect on the dynamic agency of water which, on a broad level, led me to understand the watershed the city of Bolzano is part of, and the value of the river Talvera/Talfer in that regard.

Exploring the river made me aware of sudden variations on the river flow called hydropeaking, a phenomenon caused by hydroelectric power generation that mainly affects fish species, but also brings to light the controlled nature of local rivers.

On that matter, slow but consistent efforts have restored some stretches of rivers, which has not only mitigated hydropeaking events but, in the case of the Talvera/Talfer, has given more space for locals to access the river and stay by its waters.


 

Resonance

 

Engaging locals

In a rushing race towards efficiency and energy self-sufficiency, reflecting on the wellbeing brought by the Talvera/Talfer with its own presence became a worthwhile challenge.

In this regard, an exercise called water talks was carried out in local bars to explore locals' relation with water, where watercolors postcards were the means to share and portray an important sense of relief and mindfulness associated with the presence of water and the river in the city.

This led me to an exploration of the means to broaden this value, where performing and addressing locals visiting the riverbanks was in a first moment a very engaging way to share thoughts with passers-by about themselves and the river.

In a second moment, a more structured and consistent performative intervention led to a collective narration that made possible Corrente to emerge from the banks of the Talvera/Talfer.


 

A collective narration

 

Engaging a story

With the help of a recorder that was set up inside a big balloon, not only the sound of the river but the voices of those enjoying its presence from a recreational or a spiritual point of view were recorded, while the balloon was carried up and downriver.

Once these voices merged in an audio track, this collective narration was spread along the city with the help of the same balloon used to collect the voices of river lovers, yet, this time, it was equiped with an mp3 player and headphones set.

This artefact was spread over streets and other public places of the city of Bolzano that had an historical relation with water for locals to listen to Corrente

In a subsequent moment, the collective narration contained in this artefact came back to the current of the Talfer/Talvera, let itself go down river towards the Eisarck/Isarco and then the Etsch/Adige, to finally arrive and be hosted by the Fucina Museo Project, at the Civic Museum of Rovereto, a city with rivers that are fully embanked as they pass the town.

In such a way, a reminder that it is possible to have more space for humans and rivers to be closer was brought to this context.
